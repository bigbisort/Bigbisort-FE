Sapodilla Cultivation in India

Sapota, is popularly known in India as chiku Hindi name. In India, chiku is cultivated for fruits that are liked all over the country. The sapota fruit is a fleshy berry, variable in shape, size, and weight (75-150g). The skin is thin, rusty brown, somewhat scurfy looking like an Irish potato, and the pulp is soft, crumbling, melting with a sandy or granular texture with 1-5 hard, black seeds.
